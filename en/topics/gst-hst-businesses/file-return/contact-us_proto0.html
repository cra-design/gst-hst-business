Questions about filing or your account

Online

In your CRA account you can:

  • Get account information
  • Send us documents using "Submit documents"
Sign in to your account
By phone

Call the CRA by phone or TTY if you:

  • Need help filing your return
  • Have questions specific to your file
Before you call

When you call, you will need to have your tax information ready to confirm your identity.

If you’re calling on behalf of someone else, you must be authorized to call on their behalf.

For details: Authorize a representative

By mail

If there is no address listed, or to mail us other documents, refer to: Find a CRA mailing address

If you have a complaint

If you are not satisfied with the service you received or you disagree with the CRA, you have different options.

  • If you are not satisfied with the service that you receive from us, refer to: Send feedback about CRA services
  • If you disagree with an assessment, determination, or decision, refer to: File an objection – goods and services tax/harmonized sales tax (GST/HST)
  • If you believe that you have been subject to reprisal, refer to: Reprisal Complaints

Feedback about the CRA's online filing services

If you have suggestions about how we can improve GST/HST NETFILE or GST/HST Internet File Transfer, or if you have feedback about filing GST/HST returns through your CRA account, please send us your thoughts.

To share your feedback, please email us at: netcom@cra-arc.gc.ca.

If you do email us, please do not share personal or confidential information such as your business information or your access code. These emails are not encrypted.

Do not expect a reply. In particular, we will not respond to questions about your own or your business's income tax situation. For questions about taxes or to make changes to your account, please contact us by phone.

Liaison officer support for small businesses

Certain small and medium-sized businesses can get help understanding their tax obligations through our Liaison Officer service. This is a voluntary program. If you participate, a liaison officer will help you:

  • Understand your tax obligations
  • Find out where you can get more information
  • Learn the common mistakes businesses make
  • Get answers to your questions

Refer to: Liaison Officer service
