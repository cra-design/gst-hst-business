The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) offers multiple methods for filing GST/HST returns.

Mandatory electronic filing requirement

For reporting periods that begin after December 31, 2023, all GST/HST registrants, with the exception of charities and selected listed financial institution (SLFIs), are required to file returns electronically.

If you continue to file a paper return, you will be charged a penalty.

Requesting an exemption from mandatory electronic filing

To request an exemption (granted on a case by case basis only), please write to the address below stating the reasons why you cannot comply.

Electronic filing methods

My Business Account (secure portal)
Allows registered users to interact electronically with various business accounts. These include GST/HST (except for GST/HST accounts administered by Revenu Qu√©bec), payroll, corporation income taxes, excise taxes, excise duties.
Represent a Client
(Secure portal) Allows registered and authorized users to access tax information on behalf of individuals and businesses, including your employer.
NETFILE
Allows registrants to file their GST/HST returns and eligible rebates directly to the CRA over the internet.
Internet File transfer
Allows registrants to file their GST/HST returns directly to the CRA over the internet using their third-party accounting software.
Electronic Data Interchange (EDI)
Allows registrants to file their GST/HST returns and pay the net tax they owe electronically through a participating Canadian financial institution.
TELEFILE
Allows registrants to file their GST/HST returns quickly using their touch-tone telephone and a toll-free number.

GST/HST filing and payment deadlines

The due date of your return is determined by your reporting period.To determine the deadline that applies to you, refer to When to pay.

You have to file a GST/HST return even if you :

  • Have no business transactions
  • Have no net tax to remit
  • Are closing a GST/HST account (you need to file a final return)

We can charge penalties and interest on any returns or amounts we have not received by the due date .

When a due date falls on a Saturday, a Sunday, or a public holiday recognized by the CRA, your payment is on time if we receive it on the next business day.

If you are elligible to file paper returns, the personalized GST/HST return (Form GST34-2) you receive will show the due date at the top of the form.

Filing rebates with your return

If you need to file a GST/HST rebate with your return and you are unable to do it electronically, you must still file your GST/HST return electronically and mail the rebate application to the address on the rebate form no later than the day you file your electronic return.

If you are using NETFILE, you must mail the rebate application form(s) only if you are including a rebate amount on your return and you did not file the corresponding rebate form electronically.

To learn more about filing a GST/HST rebate application go to Claim a GST/HST rebate.

Filing a return with an amount owing

You can file a return with an amount owing electronically. Find out how to make a payment to the CRA.

Joint filers

If you are the coordinator of a closely related group of corporations (i.e. you have filed election GST303) and you file returns electronically, you must still send your letter of direction to your tax centre.

First Nations Tax (FNT) accounts

First Nations Tax (FNT) accounts can use GST/HST NETFILE, GST/HST TELEFILE and GST/HST Internet File Transfer. However, after filing your return, you must mail Form GST 499-1 (the corresponding FNT schedule) to the Atlantic Tax Centre.

Authorized accountants (also mentioned in access code page, one should refer to the other)

If your accountant is legally authorized to sign the paper copy of your GST/HST return, they can use NETFILE, Internet File Transfer and TELEFILE to file your return on your behalf. They will need to use your GST/HST access code.

If your accountant is not authorized to file GST/HST returns on your behalf, you can sign up for My Business Account and add your accountant as an authorized representative.
